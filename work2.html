002 : Churn Prediction Model

This study examines churn prediction in e-commerce, highlighting increased churn rates during festive seasons, especially among Gen Z and Millennials. It identifies Jakarta as a high-churn area and emphasizes the Bronze customer segment's elevated churn. Premium and cheap product categories exhibit the highest churn rates. Insights suggest targeted post-event vouchers, UI/UX enhancements for better search, and late-night Sunday promotions to potentially mitigate churn and enhance retention.

" Customer Churn Prediction for E-commerce."

Objective

The principal aim revolved around crafting a machine learning model capable of discerning customers displaying signs of potential churn. This model aimed to facilitate the implementation of proactive strategies for customer retention.

Approach

Data Collection & Preprocessing

Gathered historical customer data such as clickstreams (browsing behaviors) transaction history, customer data, and demographics from e-commerce mockup dataset.

Feature Engineering

Engineered features such as churn definition, purchase frequency, customer lifetime value, and engagement metrics. The definition of churn is determined based on the presence or absence of transactions within the last month.

Challanges

"In e-commerce customer churn prediction, imbalanced data poses a significant challenge, impacting predictive model accuracy due to unequal churned and non-churned instances. Our platform addresses this crucial imbalance by integrating SMOTE for synthetic churned customer samples and employing label encoding to convert categorical data into a numerical format. These strategies refine predictive models, ensuring more accurate churn predictions and empowering businesses with actionable insights for customer retention and optimized marketing.

Modelling

In e-commerce customer churn prediction, Logistic Regression often emerges as the optimal model due to its simplicity and effectiveness in handling binary classification tasks, especially when the relationship between features and churn is linear or transformable. After identifying Logistic Regression as the top-performing model based on metrics like accuracy and precision, fine-tuning its performance becomes essential through GridSearchCV. This process systematically explores various hyperparameters like regularization strength and penalty to optimize the model's predictive ability. Following hyperparameter tuning, evaluating the model's efficacy using K-Fold Cross-Validation becomes imperative. K-Fold CV assesses the model's performance on different subsets of the data, ensuring a more comprehensive understanding of its generalizability and robustness on unseen datasets, thus enhancing the reliability of the predictions for customer churn in e-commerce platforms.

Feature Importance

Following the optimization of the Logistic Regression model for e-commerce customer churn prediction, analyzing feature importance involves examining coefficients to identify impactful features. Higher coefficients signify greater feature importance. Additionally, exploring feature frequencies helps pinpoint recurrent factors influencing churn, offering actionable insights for reducing churn rates in e-commerce platforms.

Results

The feature importance analysis, highlighting transaction frequencies, emphasizes the significance of directing attention toward customer transaction behaviors. This insight prompts actionable business strategies aimed at enhancing transaction volumes. Simultaneously, delving into model visualizations facilitates a comprehensive understanding of customer behaviors influenced by these transaction frequencies. By interpreting these visual representations, businesses gain valuable insights to craft tailored strategies. These strategies aim to encourage increased transactions while addressing behaviors that potentially contribute to customer churn. This holistic approach optimizes efforts toward customer retention and business growth within the e-commerce landscape.

Insight

  • Churn rates exhibit an increase during the periods surrounding Eid al-Fitr (July) and Christmas (December).
  • The highest churn rates are observed among the Gen Z and Millennial generations.
  • Classification of customers based on RFM reveals the highest churn rate within the Bronze category.
  • Jakarta emerges as the province with the highest churn rate.
  • Among product price categories, the highest churn rates are associated with the premium category, followed by the cheap category.

Bussiness Action

  • Issuing promo voucher codes regularly aims to boost transaction frequency among new customers in the future.
  • Improving the user experience in terms of UI/UX for the search box aims to enhance user comfort and ease in accessing desired items.
  • Offering attractive discounts and promotions with specific payment options during significant events in Jakarta aims to retain customer interest and encourage repeat purchases.
  • Providing promotions during regular days, specifically late at night on Sundays, aims to boost sales.
  • Updating the mobile application's UI/UX aims to enhance customer comfort while searching for desired items.
  • Offering enticing deals to users making purchases during Christmas and Eid events, such as providing vouchers valid for 30 days post-payment, aims to enhance customer engagement.
